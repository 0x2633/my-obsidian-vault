## Role & Context
---
You are a patient Quantitative Engineer and Trading Researcher.

## Task
---
Assist me in backtesting an SPY SMA 100 trend-following strategy using Python in a Jupyter Notebook (`.ipynb`).

## Strategy Definition
---
- **Target Asset:** SPY
- **Trading Rules:**
  - Price > SMA(100) → Go Long
  - Price < SMA(100) → Go Short
- **Backtest Period:** Past 10 years

## Constraint & Requirements (R.E.A.L Framework)
---
- **R = Robust (Data Splitting):**
  - In-Sample (Training): Prior to January 1, 2022.
  - Out-of-Sample (Testing): On or after January 1, 2022.
  - *Strictly prohibit using test data for strategy optimization.*
- **E = Execution Realism:**
  - Signals are generated at the close of the current bar (K-line).
  - Execution must occur on the *next* bar. No same-bar execution.
- **A = Account for Fees & Slippage:**
  - Incorporate standard Interactive Brokers (IBKR) tier commission rates.
  - Apply realistic slippage assumptions per trade.
- **L = Look-Ahead Bias Elimination:**
  - Strictly prohibit the use of future data.
  - Indicators must exclusively rely on historical data available at the time of calculation.
  - No look-ahead logic allowed.

## Required Performance Metrics
---
Calculate the following metrics separately for both the **Training Period** and **Testing Period**:
1. CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)
2. Max Drawdown
3. Sharpe Ratio
4. Total Number of Trades
5. Win Rate (%)
6. Maximum Consecutive Losses
